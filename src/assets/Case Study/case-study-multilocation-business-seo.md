## The Problem

This multi-location service business had a large footprint — multiple branches, a recognisable brand, and a loyal customer base. But on Google, they were sitting at an average position of 32.8. That is page 3.

Page 3 on Google is essentially invisible. Studies consistently show that over 90% of searchers never go beyond page 1.

The business was running on brand loyalty and existing member referrals. But the people actively searching for their service in each location — people ready to sign up right now — were finding competitors instead.

---

## What We Did

We executed a full SEO strategy covering the brand, each location, and service-specific queries.

- **Brand query dominance** — consolidated and strengthened rankings for branded searches so existing customers always find the right pages
- **Location-specific SEO** — built relevance for each branch location so each one ranks locally
- **Service and intent targeting** — optimised for high-intent queries from people actively looking to sign up
- **Content strategy** — diversified the content to capture a wider range of relevant searches
- **Technical foundation** — ensured the site could support ranking growth across multiple locations

---

## The Results

The position improvement in this case study is one of the most dramatic in our portfolio.

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Average Google position | 32.8 | 13 | **+19.8 positions** |
| 6-month clicks | 5,950 | 6,200 | +4.2% |

Going from position 32 to position 13 means moving from page 3 to the top of page 1. The impression growth of 50,000 additional monthly views means the brand now appears in front of significantly more potential customers — and that number compounds as rankings continue to improve.

When SEO works properly, it puts your business in front of people at the exact moment they are ready to become a customer. That is the difference between organic search and every other marketing channel.
